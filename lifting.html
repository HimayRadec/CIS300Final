Lifting

Splits

In order to make progress in the gym it is necessary to have a plan before going in. One of the simplest ways is to create a workout split. A workout split is simply a way of dividing your training sessions into different body regions. There is no "one size fits all" for the best workout split. But splits are adjusted depending on how many days a week you plan on training.

Lifting Terminology

  • Repetition - A complete range of motion of a particular exercise, also known as a "rep".
  • Set - Completeing a certain number of repitions in a row without stopping.
  • Volume - The product of weight x reps x sets.
  • Drop Set - Doing repitions with the same weight until failure then lowering the weight and continuing till failure again.
  • Super Set - Doing 2 different exercise without resting in between.
  • Spotting - Watching an individual during their set in order to ensure safety.
  • Compound Exercise - An exercise that works out several muscle groups at once.
  • Isolation Exercise - An exercise that works an individual muscle group at once.
  • Progressive Overload - Progressively adding more weight or reps to the same exercise each week.
